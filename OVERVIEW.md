# Aetherbourne Overview

**Aetherbourne** is a unified simulation framework for emergent world-building where reality arises from interconnected systems rather than scripted events.

## Core Concept
A living ecosystem where entities (creatures, NPCs, flora, fauna) possess perception, memory, belief, and agency. Every action flows through: Reality → Perception → Memory → Belief → Intent → Decision → Action → Feedback.

## Key Systems
- **Entity Simulation** — Characters with stats (Might, Lithe, Vigil, Grit, Vigor) and core needs (Hunger, Thirst, Warmth, Health, Social, Fulfillment, etc.)
- **Perception & Memory** — Each entity forms subjective reality based on awareness, past experiences, and relationships
- **Environmental Generation** — Procedurally created biomes with geology, flora, fauna, climate, and evolving conditions
- **Behavior & Culture** — Entities make autonomous decisions based on their brain state, goals, and social bonds
- **Item & Interaction Systems** — Objects with properties, durability, and affordances that enable emergent gameplay
- **Calendar & Weather** — Realistic time progression with seasons, weather patterns, and day/night cycles
- **Genetics & Evolution** — Fauna breed, mutate, and adapt to their environment over generations

## Technology
Rust-based simulation engine (Cargo.toml) with comprehensive system documentation and data schema for complete mechanical specification.

## Status
Phase 1 & 2 complete: Core schema documentation finished, mechanical systems fully specified and refactored for executable logic.
